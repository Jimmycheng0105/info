About

Jimmy Wei-Chun Cheng is a designer, researcher, and educator working at the intersection of architecture, artificial intelligence, and media. He is currently a faculty member at the School of Architecture at Carnegie Mellon University.

Prior to joining CMU, he taught studios, workshops, and seminars at the Boston Architectural College, the Rhode Island School of Design, and the Architectural Association Visiting School Seoul. From 2019 to 2021, he practiced in Tokyo as a project designer with Junya Ishigami + Associates and Sou Fujimoto Architects.

Cheng’s research investigates how technique, medium, and technology transform architectural design processes, with a particular focus on multimodal and conversational AI. His work examines how large language models, generative image systems, and search-driven platforms mediate between information, bodies, climate, and architectural form—from domestic interiors to urban configurations.

Drawing on semiotics, media theory, cybernetics, and simulation, his projects often take the form of tools and interfaces that turn archives, dialogue, and environmental data into feedback for designers.
